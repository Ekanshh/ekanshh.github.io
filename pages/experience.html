Work experience

Max Planck Institute for Neurobiology of Behavior (MPINB), Bonn, Germany

Guest Researcher (Apr 2025 - Present)

Currently working as a guest researcher at the MPINB, continuing research activities in a voluntary capacity following the conclusion of my previous group due to funding constraints.

In this role, I am building upon my existing work on the fly pick-and-place system developed during my prior position as a research assistant, enhancing its capabilities to better accommodate diverse experimental scenarios.

Research Assistant — Robotics (Aug 2023 - Mar 2025)

Collaborated with the Neural Circuits research group at the MPINB to develop a vision-guided robotic platform, specifically designed for automated fruit fly handling in neuroscience research experiments.

My work focused on designing and implementing a fly pick-and-place system that integrated multiple technical components, including: (i) camera-based system calibration, (ii) 3D Tool Center Point tracking, (iii) 3D fly skeleton tracking, and (iv) closed-loop robot control.

The system was subsequently validated in controlled experimental settings, enabling precise manipulation of fruit flies.

Noyes Technologies GmbH, Munich, Germany

Robotics Working Student (Apr 2022 - Jun 2023)

Working remotely as a robotics working student, I contributed to developing and maintaining software modules for robotic fleet task generation, consistently integrating new features in alignment with the company's product roadmap.

I successfully integrated NoyesStorage with a partner's mobile robot platform, which enabled a comprehensive demonstration of end-to-end warehouse automation at the LogiMAT 2022 trade fair in Stuttgart, showcasing our solutions to industry professionals and potential stakeholders.

Additionally, I supported system testing and collaborated with cross-functional teams to identify and resolve technical issues using agile development practices.

Robotics Software Developer Intern (Aug 2021 - Mar 2022)

As the company's first intern, I had the opportunity to contribute to the early-stage development of NoyesStorage, an innovative modular robotic storage system designed for warehouse automation applications.

I supported a proof-of-concept project for an IoT-based temperature monitoring system, essential for validating the cooling functionality of specialized refrigerated storage units within the NoyesStorage system.

As my internship progressed, I took on key software modules supporting robotic fleet management operations, contributing directly to the core functionality of the system.

Sterlite Power, New Delhi, India

Robotics Intern (Sep 2018 - May 2019)

Contributed to the prototype development of Skyrob, an innovative robotic system designed for automated Optical Ground Wire (OPGW) stringing and inspection on high-voltage power transmission lines.

This groundbreaking project was part of Sterlite Power's pioneering initiative to introduce robotic technology in India's power transmission sector for the first time, with the primary objectives of enhancing worker safety and operational efficiency in hazardous high-voltage environments.

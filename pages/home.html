About me

Hi, I'm Ekansh Sharma !

I'm currently pursuing my master's degree in Autonomous Systems at Hochschule Bonn-Rhein-Sieg. Alongside, I work as a guest researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Neurobiology of Behavior, where I'm developing a custom vision-guided robotic platform to automate the task of precise manipulation of fruit flies for neuroscience research.

You can find more details in my and sections.

I've been fortunate to work across both academia and industry, collaborating with multicultural teams on multidisciplinary projects spanning robot manipulation, navigation, perception, planning and adaptive compliant control. I've worked with a variety of robots, from fixed-base manipulators to mobile platforms, both in simulation and on real hardware.

And yes, when the situation called for it, I've rolled up my sleeves to get hands-on with: designing and soldering custom PCBs, interfacing sensors and actuators, programming microcontrollers, setting up experimental rigs, and even briefly working with PLCs to develop custom automation solutions (SPM) in a short-term gig. Personally, I see it as all part of the everyday roboticist's life, but honestly, I'm more passionate about the software and algorithmic side of robotics.

My core research interest lies in making robots intelligent, enabling them to adapt and make informed decisions while executing complex tasks — ultimately mimicking human-like cognitive capabilities through data-efficient methods. Most recently, in my master's thesis research, I have developed a model-based framework that enables a robot to estimate the optimal kinematic configuration of articulated objects (such as doors, drawers, etc.) during physical interaction, and subsequently use this information for informed motion planning and compliant control, resulting in more efficient and robust manipulation of these objects.

Check out my section for more details on my thesis and other cool things I've worked on. Also, feel free to reach out if you'd like to discuss potential collaborations or just want to say hello! :)
